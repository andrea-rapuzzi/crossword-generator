## Design Context

### Users
**Two distinct audiences:**
1. **Admin** — FDL editors, ~monthly use, creating crosswords from article URLs. Internal tool; functional efficiency matters but it should still feel premium.
2. **Player** — FDL website readers, consumer-grade public-facing crossword game. This is the flagship surface.

### Brand Personality
Fine Dining Lovers: editoriale, autentico, premium.

References: finedininglovers.com, Ffern, ItalySegreta — all share editorial confidence, generous white space, serif-led typography, warm-not-precious tone.

Anti-references: anything that feels like a SaaS dashboard, startup-blue, or generic food app.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Light only
- **Mood**: An Italian food magazine printed on heavy stock — warm beige ground, dark navy anchors, confident serif headings
- **Typography**: Rufina (display serif — high contrast thin/thick, editorial Italian feeling) paired with Jost (geometric humanist sans — clean, contemporary, pairs beautifully with high-contrast serifs)
- **Color**: Warm beige background (#f0ebe5), deep navy (#131e2e), restrained cyan accent (#62c6e9) for interactive states only

### Design Principles
1. **Editorial confidence** — let typography carry weight; avoid decorative noise
2. **Authentic grid** — crossword cells are sharp-cornered, no border-radius; this is a traditional puzzle form
3. **Restraint on accent colors** — cyan and red only for clear interactive/status meaning, never decoration
4. **Consumer-grade player** — the playing experience should feel like a NYT-quality crossword, not a prototype
5. **Warmth without whimsy** — the beige palette is warm but the overall feel is refined, not playful
